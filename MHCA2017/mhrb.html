Mental Health Review Boards

Mental Health Review Board, under  Mental Healthcare Act 2017 is the actual authority which ensures rights of persons with mental illness are not violated. It is formed by state mental health authority, for each district in the state, and comprises of district judge and/or magistrate, psychiatrists, and persons with mental illness etc. which may vary depending upon each state. If the rights of any persons are being or have been violated in a mental health establishment, he/she must make an application to the respective district mental health review board (see below for details).

With Mental Health Review Boards, following applications can be made: 

1. To register, review, alter, modify or cancel an advance directive
2. To appoint a nominated representative
3. To review admission in a mental health establishment and order discharge/stop treatment. 
4. To file complaints regarding poor or deficient care and services in the mental health establishment
5. To request full disclosure when medical records are not disclosed completely by the medical officer
